In H2 2020, some countries are seizing on Asia’s relative success in containing the virus to establish travel bubbles to allow limited numbers of foreign tourists and essential workers to visit. Singapore is only just getting started!


In July 2020, Close to 30,000 passengers arrive and depart from Changi Airport weekly 🎉 🎏 🌍

Singapore’s government remains wary of a new outbreak, even after lifting a lockdown in June. Singapore Airlines (SIA) and SilkAir have since increased their flight frequency to operate at approximately 6% of original scheduled capacity.

Looking at flight data split across the airlines, SIA continues to drive majority of flight traffic to and from Changi Airport.



SIA is already flying beyond South East Asia to Europe and United States! 🦁 🌅 🍷

There are two way flights available across 5 regions: South-east Asia, North Asia, South-west Pacific, Europe and the United States.

South East Asia come in first in volume as we see more frequent flight schedules with smaller planes. Flights to Europe happen less frequently but to more destinations and feature larger planes to cope with longer haul flights.


South East Asia - Kuala Lumpur, Jakarta, Medan, Cebu, Manila, Bangkok
Europe - Copenhagen, Paris, Frankfurt, Amsterdam, Barcelona, Zurich, London (Heathrow)
South West Pacific - Adelaide, Brisbane, Melbourne, Sydney, Auckland, Christchurch
North Asia - Chong Qing, Shanghai, Hong Kong, Osaka, Tokyo (Narita), Seoul
America - Los Angeles



Where do our inbound and outbound passengers come from and go to? 🏝 🌆 🍁

SIA and SilkAir are flying across 25 cities in 18 countries in July 2020. As countries continue to lift their lockdowns, destinations will continue to grow in number as SIA said it will continue to adjust its capacity to match the demand for international air travel.

Most frequently travelled locations are Jakarta, Sydney and Hongkong/Kuala Lumpur in July.



As our travel industry continues to recover, Singaporeans will continue to dream about trips overseas... Stay safe everyone! 🥕 🥝 🥑
